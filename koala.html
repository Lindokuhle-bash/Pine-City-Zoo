KOALA

The koala (Phascolarctos cinereus) is a tree-dwelling marsupial native to Australia. Known for their fluffy ears, spoon-shaped nose, and relaxed nature, koalas are one of Australia's most iconic animals.

Koalas live in eucalyptus forests and spend most of their time sleeping in the treetops. They feed almost exclusively on eucalyptus leaves, which are low in nutrients, causing them to sleep up to 20 hours a day to conserve energy.
